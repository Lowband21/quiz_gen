Question: What is a key difference between tuples and lists in Python and when might you use one over the other?
Possible Answers: 
a) Tuples and lists are the same, there is no difference.
b) Tuples are mutable and lists are immutable, use tuples when you need your data to change.
c) Lists are mutable and tuples are immutable, use tuples when you need your data to be unchangeable.
d) Lists are defined using parentheses and tuples use square brackets, use lists when you need to slice your data.
